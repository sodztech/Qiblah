The Blessings of Jumu'ah

Sunnahs & Practices for the Best Day of the Week

☽
خَيْرُ يَوْمٍ طَلَعَتْ عَلَيْهِ الشَّمْسُ يَوْمُ الْجُمُعَةِ

"The best day on which the sun has risen is Friday. On it Adam was created, on it he was made to enter Paradise, on it he was expelled from it, and the Hour will not be established except on Friday."

Sahih Muslim
Sunnahs of Jumu'ah
1
Take a Bath (Ghusl)
Perform a complete bath before attending Jumu'ah prayer. This is compulsory for every Muslim who has attained the age of puberty.
"The taking of a bath on Friday is compulsory for every Muslim who has attained the age of puberty and also cleaning of teeth with Siwak and using perfume, if available."Sahih Bukhari
2
Use Siwak
Clean your teeth with a Siwak (miswak) before heading to the masjid on Friday.
3
Wear Clean & Presentable Clothes
Wear clean, presentable and preferably new clothes before the Friday prayer.
"Put on your adornment on every occasion of prayer."Surah Al-A'raf (7:31)
4
Apply Perfume / Oud
Apply perfume or Oud before leaving for the masjid. Ensure it is non-alcoholic.
5
Cut Your Nails
Clipping the nails is among the acts of fitra (natural disposition).
"Five are the demonstrations of fitra: circumcision, removing hair beneath the navel, trimming the mustache, trimming the nails, and removing hair under the arms."Narrated by Abu Hurayrah (R.A.)
6
Read Surah Al-Kahf
Recite Surah Al-Kahf on Friday. You may read:
First 10 Ayahs Last 10 Ayahs ✦ Ideally — Complete Surah Al-Kahf
"Whoever reads Surah Al-Kahf on Friday, he will be illuminated with light between the two Fridays."Narrated by Al-Haakim | Graded Saheeh by Al-Albaani
7
Send Abundant Durood upon the Prophet ﷺ
Increase your sending of blessings and salutations upon the Prophet Muhammad ﷺ throughout Friday.
"The best of your days is Friday. On that day Adam was created; on that day he died; on that day the Trumpet will be blown... So send a lot of blessings upon me, for your blessings will be shown to me."Abu Dawood | Graded Saheeh by Ibn Al-Qayyim
✦ The Golden Hour

Make Abundant Du'a Between Asr & Maghrib

"Friday has twelve hours. There is one hour during which if a Muslim asks Allah anything, Allah will give it to him, so find it during the last hour after Asr."

After Asr → Until Maghrib Adhaan
9
Go to the Masjid as Early as Possible
The earlier you arrive, the greater the reward.
"When it is Jumu'ah, the Angels stand at the gate of the masjid and keep on writing the names of the persons coming in succession... The example of the one who enters earliest is that of one offering a camel in sacrifice; the next, a cow; then a ram; then a chicken; then an egg."Sahih Al-Bukhari
10
Walk to the Masjid
Walk to the masjid instead of driving when possible — every step elevates your rank and wipes away sins.
"He who purifies himself in his house and then walks to one of the houses of Allah for performing an obligatory Salah, one step will wipe out his sins and another will elevate his rank in Jannah."Muslim
11
Don't Separate Two People
Do not step over others or push between two people already seated. Sit in an empty space.
12
Listen to the Khutbah Attentively
Remain completely silent during the Khutbah — do not talk, whisper, or gesture to others. Even telling someone to "be quiet" is considered a lapse.
"Whoever says to his friend while the Imam is delivering the Khutbah on Friday 'keep quiet' has also committed an absurdity."Muslim & Bukhari
جَامِعُ السُّنَن

"If a man takes a bath on Friday, cleans himself as much as he can, oils his hair, applies perfume available in his house, sets forth for the mosque, does not separate two people to make a seat for himself, performs as many prayers as written for him, remains silent when the Imam speaks — for his sins between that Friday and the following Friday will be forgiven."

Al-Bukhari
